Learning to Create Even When Inspiration Feels Far Away

Sometimes creating doesn’t begin with motivation. Sometimes it begins with showing up anyway.

Lately, I’ve been thinking a lot about what it means to create when you don’t necessarily feel inspired. A lot of people imagine art as this magical process where ideas just appear, but the truth is that creativity can be messy, uncertain, and even frustrating at times.

There are days when everything flows naturally, and there are other days when even starting feels difficult. I think that’s part of the process too. Growth doesn’t only happen in the moments where everything looks polished. It also happens in the quiet moments where you keep going, experiment, and allow yourself to make something imperfect.

For me, art has always been more than just the final piece. It’s a way to process ideas, tell stories, and better understand myself. Whether I’m working on prints, animation, design, or something completely new, I’m always learning through the act of making.

I wanted this blog to be a space where I can share some of those thoughts honestly—creative highs, creative lows, lessons I’m learning, and the ideas that keep me moving forward.

Creativity is not always about feeling ready. Sometimes it’s about being willing.

Thanks for reading and being part of the journey.
